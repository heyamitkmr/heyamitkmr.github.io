### **About me**

I'm currently exploring the intersection of **creation, learning, and connection.**

More than anything, I want to make: articles, essays, books, software, systems, and ideas that help people think differently about their lives and work.

I work best in 2-3 focused blocks per day with ideally not more than 2-3 longer term goals over a period of 3-6 months, with weekly calibrations. 

---

### **Recent explorations**

<span style="font-size: 16px">_More **updated version of what I'm doing now** here on [my now page](/now)_</span>

Exploring the path towards maximising impact while retaining creative independence - through writing, building products, and creating community. The focus is on freedom: control over my time, being useful to others, and building things that exist independent of me.

Some recent work:

- **[CareerMap](https://careermap.nextfive.in/)**: Making non-obvious career paths visible. Built on the premise that [people can only want what they can see exists](https://nextfive.xyz/blog/2026/04/11/careermap.html). The first vertical: independent and micro-economy careers.

- **[Orbit](https://nextfive.xyz/orbit)**: An experiment in redistributing network capital. Financial capital has microfinance. Skills have vocational training. But network capital (access to the people who open doors) has no redistribution infrastructure. Testing whether that can change, starting in Bangalore.

- **[Guide](https://guide.nextfive.in/)**: A tool for managing goals, tasks, and thinking. Built for myself first.

- **[NextFive Publisher](https://nextfive.xyz/projects/)**: A single-file tool for publishing to Jekyll/GitHub Pages blogs. So I could start posting [short bits](https://nextfive.xyz/bits/) on the go, like Twitter. Part of a collection of [small, useful web apps](https://blog.nextfive.in/products/). 

- **[Writing](https://nextfive.xyz/writing)**: Mostly because it helps me think. Also practicing thematic writing [on Substack](https://earlynotes.substack.com/), with essays on how systems work, why some problems stay invisible, and what happens when you try to change that. But I'm still exploring the identity of EarlyNotes.
- **Listening and sharing stories**: With a bunch of interesting folks at [Listeners' Circle](https://www.meetup.com/listeners-circle-bangalore/) and [Creative Mornings Bangalore](https://www.creativemornings.com/cities/BLR).

### **How I Work**

<p>I prefer <b>one-on-one conversations</b> over group hangouts. Quality over quantity of connection.</p>
<p>I'm deliberate about my choices. I don't believe in "I can't help the way I am." Life is something you design, not something that happens to you.</p>
<p>I hate wasting time. Hours matter. I imagine my time as valuable - worth protecting - and ask myself what's worth that investment. Though I often feel like I make wrong choices. But I have also noticed a tendency to judge myself harshly. I'm working on both. </p>

---

### **What matters to me**

- **Autonomy**: Control over my time and what I work on.
- **Usefulness**: My work should help people in some immediate, real way.
- **Durability**: Creating things that outlast me. Writing, software, ideas that stand independent of my presence.
- **Growth**: Always learning. Always pushing into unfamiliar territory.
- **Genuine connection**: Real conversations with people doing interesting things.

### **What doesn't matter**

- Money as a metric of success _(i.e. beyond financial freedom to choose my work and occasional indulgences)_.
- Fitting in or following the expected path.

---

### **Life philosophy**
<p>I'm skeptical of fixed beliefs - the opposite of what I believe is probably also true. But currently trying to be more aligned with these principles:</p>

- **Action over planning.** Move fast from thinking to doing. You discover what matters by trying. Something I've struggled with. Getting better.
- **Focus.** Say no ruthlessly. Protect time for what matters. Working on this.
- **Iterative improvement.** Done is better than perfect. Iterate toward better.
- **Be useful.** Help people. Make things people need.
- **[Dailyish](https://earlynotes.substack.com/p/showing-up-dailyish) is consistent.** You don't need perfection. 3-4 times a week is consistent enough.
